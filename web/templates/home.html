Infrastructure

India's open health infrastructure

UHI is India's open protocol for health services -- the same idea as UPI, applied to healthcare. Aayu is built directly on this stack, which means your parent's records from any ABDM-linked hospital or lab can flow into one place with their consent.

ABHA Health ID

One ID for every Indian

Ayushman Bharat Health Account -- a 14-digit identifier that links a person's health records across all hospitals, labs, and pharmacies. 847 million IDs created. Your parent's Apollo visit, the local clinic prescription, and the diagnostic report are all accessible through one number.

UHI Unified Health Interface

Open protocol, not a closed app

UHI is the open network layer that lets any compliant app discover doctors, book consultations, and access health records -- without being locked into one hospital's portal. Governed by the National Health Authority. Think of it as the routing layer beneath every health interaction.

HIE-CM Consent Manager

Patient controls every access

Before any record moves, the patient approves the request -- what data, from which provider, to whom. The Health Information Exchange and Consent Manager is the layer that enforces this. Aayu uses it to ensure family members see only what the patient has explicitly permitted.

Today V2 Roadmap: ABDM certified

ABHA-linked consent and audit trail

Every data access in Aayu is logged to a consent table, tied to the patient's ABHA ID. The HIE-CM layer ensures no family member or doctor can pull records without an explicit approval event on record. Today this lives in Supabase with full timestamps and role attribution.

Records are versioned and never deleted. When a prescription changes, the old version is marked superseded -- the full history is preserved with its ABHA ID linkage intact. In V2, Aayu will integrate directly with the ABDM HIE-CM gateway, so consent artifacts are issued and managed by India's national health consent infrastructure -- the same system trusted by 45 crore ABHA holders.

Security and Privacy

We take health data as seriously as medicine.

01

You control every access

Every record transfer requires your explicit consent via ABDM's HIE-CM Consent Manager. No data moves without you approving it -- not to doctors, not to insurance companies, not to us.

02

Built on government infrastructure

Aayu uses India's National Health Authority (NHA) gateway. Your records link to ABHA -- the same system used by AIIMS and 438,000+ hospitals across the country.

03

DPDP Act 2023 compliant

Health data is Sensitive Personal Data under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023. We process it with explicit purpose consent, retain only what's needed, and provide full deletion on request.

04

Your records are never deleted

When a prescription changes, the old version is marked superseded -- not erased. Every version is preserved with a timestamp and its full ABHA ID linkage intact.

05

Encrypted, role-gated, audit-logged

Data is encrypted at rest and in transit (AES-256, TLS 1.3). Only family members you explicitly add can view records. Every access is logged with a timestamp and actor ID.
